Tips and Tricks to Overcome Aphasia by Yourself

Please practise every day, ten minutes per day or an hour every day. You will see your speaking, reading and writing results over time.

"Compare yourself to who you were yesterday, not to who someone else is today" - Jordan Peterson.

Read Out Loud

Read a children's book.

Read a novel.

Read an article that interests you (a physical magazine or an online article, it doesn't matter either one).

Words Starting With a Letter

Write down a word on paper or a text editor like Notepad (Microsoft Windows), Word (Microsoft Windows or Apple Mac) or TextEdit (Apple Mac). Keep going!

Describe The Scene

Google "describe the scene", pick one and describing the scene out loud.

Grammarly Website

I have used Grammarly after my stroke and a game-changer for me. I love a grammar check, especially while I'm writing an email or text message.

If you want to improve your writing, the official website is here.

Conversation Between You and Your Amazon Alexa

A few lines to say include "what is the weather today", "what is the date today" and 'what is the temperature today".

Attend the Toastmasters Sessions

Check out the official website to see the sessions near to you.

I have been to the Toastmasters in Manchester in the UK. I know, it's very daunting the first time you attend, but everyone understands you and the session goal to improve everyone's speech.

If You Like Coffee or Tea!

Go out to a coffee shop and order something you like, "a latte, please" for example.

Finally...

Go out for a walk or something to relax you, or you love. You deserve it!

"You may never know what results come of your actions, but if you do nothing, there will be no results" - Mahatma Gandhi.
